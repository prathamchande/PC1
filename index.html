What is UI / UX Design?

User interface/user experience design is one of the hottest areas in technology today. But what exactly is it? Read our introduction and learn about the people who practice it and the tools used.

Research

Research is a key UX design skill. Designers must make actionable insights from the data they collect, both in the initial phases and when testing with real people.

Problem-solving

UX designers explore many different approaches to solving a specific user problem in a process known as “ideate.”

Communication

Communication is crucial as UX design is a highly collaborative process. UX designers also need a level of empathy that lets them look at a product from the user’s point of view.

UX strategy

So, a UX strategy should present a clear statement of the product vision and the results that we want to achieve for the business and end-users

Creating a UX Strategy

Evaluate the current state

It is very useful if there is a business plan that you can rely on. But even if there is none, you will have to fill in the gaps of missing data through interviews with stakeholders.

  • Common tasks that the business should solve. For example, test the effectiveness of a business model, win over users’ loyalty, retain customers, and so on. Understanding such basic goals allows you to set the right focus from the very beginning. UX strategy cannot (and should not) be created without considering the needs of the business.
  • Problems that you already know about. For example, user churn increases, annual recurring revenue does not grow.
  • The resources you have.

Set your goals, UX vision, and proof of concept

Once it becomes clear what is happening with the product and with the business right now, it's time to think about how you would like the product to be perceived in the future.

  • We adopt the strategy for a certain period of time. It can be 6 months, after which we hold a retrospective and discuss whether it needs to be updated or you can take a longer period
  • If you still don’t have your user personas defined, you can think of your target audience (still, it is not that effective).
  • focus area is to discuss with the team for whom exactly they solve this or that issue or create a new feature.

Services and products

Focus on services/products is relevant for big companies, in case you have a small project your focus area will be parts of a product or interaction stages. To find this focus area we should get back to the two first steps - business issues and goals.

  • If the product performs only one function there probably will be no problems with the allocation of resources. But what if there is more than one feature, and besides, users at different interaction stages use different functions, your team faces a problem of what to do first: design onboarding, work on increasing conversions to paid subscriptions, or develop new uiUxDesign.

Develop a plan

Everything we talked about before had a single purpose: to understand what actions to take in order to reach your final goal.

  • Conduct user research
  • Develop user personas (or validate existing ones).
  • Create a prototype.
  • Start usability testing on prototypes.
  • Develop a design system.
  • Conduct the content audit and align it with the strategy.
